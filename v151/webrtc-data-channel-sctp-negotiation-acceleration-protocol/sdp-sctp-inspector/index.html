what SNAP adds

WebRTC data channels are signalled in SDP with a media section like m=application <port> UDP/DTLS/SCTP webrtc-datachannel, plus a=sctp-port and a=max-message-size. SNAP defines a new media-level attribute on that same section:

m=application 9 UDP/DTLS/SCTP webrtc-datachannel
a=sctp-port:5000
a=max-message-size:262144
a=setup:actpass
a=fingerprint:sha-256 …
a=sctp-init:AQAAHols3R0AUAAA/////+B5ZR3AAAAEgAgABoLA   ← SNAP: base64 SCTP INIT params

The a=sctp-init value is a base64-encoded SCTP INIT chunk. Because both peers exchange it in the offer/answer they already send, the SCTP association is agreed before DTLS finishes, removing the separate INIT round trips. This inspector probes the offer your browser actually produced — if the attribute is absent, your build negotiates the classic way, and the demo says so rather than pretending.
