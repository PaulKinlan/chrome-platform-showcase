Why the size matters

  1. Middleboxes that hard-code "TLS ClientHello fits in one packet" reject oversized hellos. Chrome's rollout caused real-world breakage when the ClientHello exceeded ~1.5KB.
  2. Every connection pays the cost, even to servers that don't speak PQ — so Chrome usually advertises both X25519MLKEM768 and a backup X25519 share, paying an extra 32B for the backup.
  3. On mobile networks, the extra round-trip-trigger packet is measurable; on connection setup, it's typically <1ms.
