How SoftNavigation entries work

Each client-side route change that the browser heuristically detects as a navigation produces a SoftNavigationEntry in the Performance Timeline. The entry carries a navigationId that is also stamped on any LCP, INP, or CLS entries observed during that route's lifetime — giving you per-route Core Web Vitals without any manual instrumentation of the URL.

The duration field is the wall-clock time from the triggering user interaction to the largest content paint of the new route. A route with a long duration is a candidate for lazy-loading or skeleton screens.
