Anchor Carousel

The carousel use case the chromestatus entry calls out. Anchors inside a scroll-target-group: auto nav become scroll markers, and :target-current highlights the active slide. Real HTML anchors — accessible, tab-navigable, no JavaScript.

Slide one

The chromestatus motivation: ::scroll-marker pseudo-elements work for this pattern, but as pseudo-elements they can't be standalone anchors, can't be focused independently, and can't carry independent ARIA.

Slide two

scroll-target-group turns regular <a> elements into scroll markers. Tab order works. Screen readers announce them as links.

Slide three

The currently-visible slide's marker matches :target-current, so you can style it differently with one selector and no JavaScript.

Slide four

Smooth scroll behaviour is inherited from the scroller. Clicking the marker animates into view.

Slide five

Outside Chrome 140 these are just normal anchor links — the navigation still works, but :target-current won't restyle.

Scroll the carousel or click a marker dot. In Chrome 140 the active dot grows and fills with black via :target-current.
